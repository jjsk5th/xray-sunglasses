Office Attendant App

Improving Internal Process

Goal

Decrease late or missed shifts. Improve the quality of work completed and increase Attendant accountability. More accurate shift change/call-off data.

Research consisted of

  • – Interviews and process mapping with HR and Operations.
  • – Office Attendant interviews.
  • – Usability testing of prototype.

Requirements

  • – Office Attendants need a way to see their scope of work.
  • – Attendants need to be able to see their schedule, the ability to call off the shift or notify staff of being late.
  • – Leave a note summarizing the shift for Operations and the Office Manager.
  • – See details about the client site.
  • – See their paycheck information.
  • – Contact info other members of the team.
  • – See their kudos.

Team

  • – Jack Kent - Design
  • – Lauren Polkow - Product
  • – Josh Beckman - Engineer

Shift Schedule

The OfficeLuv Attendants had no easy way to see their schedule. This caused several business and process problems including missed shifts, running late, being locked out, not knowing what they were expected to do at each client.

Running Late

The attendants would frequently contact the client directly to notify them they would be late. This left OfficeLuv in the dark about what was really going on from a service standpoint and we needed a way to be improve our on-time rates.
